Medical Certification

DGCA Medical Assessment Process

Every aspiring pilot must complete DGCA medical certification before progressing through pilot training and licensing.

Class 2 Medical

Required before joining a DGCA-approved Flying Training Organisation (FTO) and obtaining a Student Pilot Licence (SPL).

Common Assessments

  • Vision examination
  • Hearing assessment
  • Blood and urine tests
  • ECG
  • Physical examination
  • Medical history review
Recommended Stage Before Flight School Admission

Class 1 Medical

Mandatory before issuance of a Commercial Pilot Licence (CPL).

Common Assessments

  • Advanced Vision Testing
  • Audiometry
  • Cardiovascular Evaluation
  • Treadmill Test (TMT)
  • Echocardiography
  • Laboratory Investigations
Recommended Stage As Early As Possible During Training
Reality Check

Many students begin flight training and invest significant amounts before completing their Class 1 Medical assessment.

If a medical limitation is identified later, it may affect future licensing eligibility and training progression.

Ace Aviator Recommendation

Complete your Class 1 Medical at the earliest possible stage before making major training investments.

Medical certification is governed entirely by DGCA regulations and is subject to individual assessment by DGCA-authorized medical examiners and medical boards.

Aviation Terms

Aviation Glossary

Learn commonly used aviation terms, abbreviations, and pilot language used during training and airline operations.

A
ATC
Air Traffic Control

The service responsible for managing aircraft movement safely in airspace and airports.

C
CPL
Commercial Pilot License

A professional pilot license allowing pilots to fly aircraft commercially.

F
FTO
Flight Training Organization

DGCA-approved aviation schools where pilot training and flying lessons are conducted.

P
PIC
Pilot in Command

The pilot legally responsible for the operation and safety of the aircraft during flight.
