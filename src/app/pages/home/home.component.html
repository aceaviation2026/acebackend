Most CPL programs in India practically take around 18–24 months due to weather, instructor availability, aircraft maintenance, and DGCA processing timelines.

01
0 – 6 Months

Ground School & Weather Delays

Students begin DGCA ground classes and initial flight training, but heavy monsoon conditions and reduced visibility can temporarily reduce flying opportunities in many regions across India.

02
6 – 12 Months

Flight Training & Instructor Availability

Students continue building flying hours, simulator sessions, and solo practice. Instructor shortages at some academies may create scheduling backlogs and slower flying slot availability.

03
12 – 18 Months

Flying Hours & Aircraft Maintenance

Students work toward required CPL flying hours, cross-country training, and technical preparation. Aircraft maintenance schedules and spare part availability may temporarily affect operations.

04
18 – 24 Months

DGCA Exams & CPL Completion

Students complete DGCA examinations, medical clearances, documentation, RTR preparation, and final CPL processing. Most students practically complete CPL training within 18–24 months in India.

Type Rating for A320 / B737

After obtaining a Commercial Pilot License (CPL), most students complete an Aircraft Type Rating on aircraft such as the Airbus A320 or Boeing B737. This advanced airline-specific training is typically required for airline recruitment and professional airline operations.
